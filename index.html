What does our research do?
Why connect this to AI?

AI is becoming part of education, search, and everyday tools for families. But most AI is trained mostly on adult language, not child language.

That matters because children do not speak like small adults. Younger children often give shorter answers, add fewer reasons, and introduce fewer new ideas on their own. If AI does not recognize those patterns, it may misunderstand what a child means or respond in ways that are too advanced.
What are we studying?

In our research, we study child language using two ideas that go beyond sentence length alone.

💬 Expansion
How much the child adds details, explanations, or reasons.
⭐ Independence
How much the child introduces ideas instead of only reacting to the adult's question.
Together, these help us measure how a child expresses ideas — not just how many words they say.
Why guess age?

In this activity, the AI makes a zero-shot age guess from the conversation alone. It is not given the child's age in advance. We use this as a simple, visible way to ask: What signals in language make someone sound younger or older?

In our research, we then use Expansion and Independence as separate measures to describe those developmental patterns more carefully. If those measures help predict age, they are capturing something meaningful about how children's language changes over time.
What information is the AI using?

The AI looks only at the conversation text. It may notice patterns like:

📝
How much detail the child gives
💡
Whether the child explains reasons
⭐
Whether the child introduces new ideas
🔗
How complex or connected the responses are
These are related to the same kinds of patterns we highlight in the research, but the game is a simple demo — not the full research method.

What this activity shows
1
Children's language development is about how they express ideas, not just how many words they use.
2
Our model looks for Expansion and Independence to detect those age-related patterns.
3
Comparing your guess with the AI helps us study where people and models see the same signals — and where they differ.
AI guess vs. our research
The AI in this activity makes a direct age guess from the conversation alone. In our research, we separately analyze child language using Expansion and Independence to describe developmental patterns more clearly.
🎙 Facilitator talking points
1
Kids do not just talk more as they get older — they explain more, connect ideas, and sometimes lead the conversation themselves.
2
In our research, we analyze child language using two concepts: Expansion, which means adding details or reasons, and Independence, which means introducing ideas on their own.
3
Most AI is trained on adult language. This research helps us design systems that work better for children too.
4
This activity also lets us compare people's judgments with AI predictions, which is part of the science.
